▸ Case Study — Prestige Mobile Cocktail Bars

From word-of-mouth
to booked online.

A Zambian mobile cocktail bar business had no web presence, no way to capture leads, and no digital proof of their service. We built everything from zero — the site, the copy, the brand voice online.

01 — Diagnosis

The brief

Prestige Mobile Cocktail Bars provides premium bar experiences for weddings, birthdays, corporate events and private celebrations across Zambia. They had a real, operating business — but zero digital presence. No website. No way for potential clients to find them, vet them, or book them online.

The Problem

A premium events business surviving entirely on referrals and word-of-mouth. No website meant no credibility for new clients who hadn't heard of them, and no way to capture enquiries at scale.

The Audience

Couples planning weddings, corporate event coordinators, and individuals organising private celebrations in Zambia — all making high-consideration, high-spend decisions.

The Stakes

Events bookings are trust-heavy. A client spending significant money on their wedding bar needs to feel confident before they ever make contact. The website had to do that qualifying work automatically.

The Deliverable

A full website build from scratch — design, development, and all copy written and deployed. Zero existing assets. Built entirely to brief.

Analyst's Note — The Credibility Gap In high-consideration purchases like event services, the website isn't where people decide to book — it's where they decide whether to even make contact. A weak or absent web presence signals risk to a buyer spending real money on an important occasion. The primary job of this site was not to sell. It was to eliminate doubt before the first conversation.

Strategic Note — Two CTAs, Two Buyer Types The "Request a Quote" CTA targets the decided buyer — someone ready to move forward. "View Gallery" targets the undecided buyer who needs more social proof before committing. Offering both captures both stages of the decision journey. A single CTA would lose one of them.
